The Biryani Map of India

Five Kingdoms, One Obsession

Each region of India guards its own biryani secret — a different rice, a different spice hand, a different ritual of dum. Hover to discover.

Tamil Nadu
Dindigul Biryani
Seeraga Samba rice cooked with cube-cut mutton, curd, and Guntur chillies. Tangier, spicier, and smaller-grained than any other.
Our Origin
Hyderabad
Kacchi Dum Biryani
Raw marinated meat layered with aged Basmati, sealed with dough and slow-cooked. The Nizami handi method in its purest form.
Iconic
Lucknow
Awadhi Biryani
Pakki dum — meat cooked separately, then layered. Subtle saffron, rose water, and ittar give it a perfumed Nawabi elegance.
Royal
Kolkata
Kolkata Biryani
The Nawab-in-exile added potato and egg to stretch rice — a colonial-era adaptation that became Bengal's most beloved comfort.
Unique
Kerala
Malabar Biryani
Jeerakasala rice, coconut ghee, and fried cashews. Arab-influenced spice routes shaped a biryani that smells of the sea and cardamom.
